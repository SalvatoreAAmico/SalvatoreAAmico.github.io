Key Findings to Surface

Insights the finished dashboard will make visible at a glance

Peak Event

January 2023 was the worst single month — ~89,000 layoffs — driven by post-holiday budget resets after 2022 Federal Reserve rate hikes.

The Big Tech Winter

November 2022 through March 2023 accounted for over 40% of all layoffs in the dataset, led by Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, and Google.

Stage vs. Severity

Post-IPO companies dominated absolute layoff counts, but Seed-stage startups had the highest percentage laid off — many were outright shut down.

Sector Exposure

Consumer and Retail were hardest hit — both heavily exposed to pandemic-era spending that reversed sharply when interest rates rose in 2022.

Data source: Layoffs.fyi — a crowd-sourced database tracking tech company layoffs worldwide since March 2020. Cited by Bloomberg, the New York Times, and CNBC. Tool: Google Looker Studio (free, browser-based). Data cleaned in Microsoft Excel and connected live via Google Sheets.
